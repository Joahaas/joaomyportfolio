Overview

The Polaroid app needed a complete redesign to appeal to modern users while maintaining the brand's nostalgic charm. I reimagined the photography experience to combine instant gratification with creative editing tools and social sharing capabilities.

The Challenge

The original Polaroid app felt outdated and failed to capture the magic of instant photography. Users wanted a seamless experience that honored Polaroid's heritage while offering modern features like filters, frames, and easy sharing.

Key Problems Identified

  • Lack of community: Users couldn’t easily explore or connect with others’ Polaroids inside the app.
  • Disconnected sharing flow: Sharing required exporting to external apps, breaking the Polaroid brand experience.
  • Limited album functionality: Existing album tools didn’t support collaborative or interactive memory walls with friends or family.
  • Static user experience: The app lacked an engaging, scrollable feed to discover and emotionally connect with others’ moments.

The Goal

Design a more social and emotionally engaging Polaroid app experience by introducing community-driven features that allow users to discover, share, and build visual memory walls together—without leaving the Polaroid ecosystem.

Research & Findings

To inform my redesign, I conducted a mix of competitive benchmarking, user interviews, and a Jobs-to-Be-Done analysis.

Benchmarking

I analyzed leading photo-sharing apps like Instagram, Tiktok, Pinterest, and Flickr to understand best practices in community-building and content discovery.

User Interviews

I interviewed 6 Polaroid users to understand their habits and frustrations with the current app.

Current User Flow

I analyzed the current user flow of the app to understand how users interact with the app.

Jobs-to-be-Done

I conducted a Jobs-to-be-Done analysis to identify the key jobs users were trying to accomplish with the app.

Key Insight

Users are highly creative and value flexibility, sometimes wanting to share spontaneous, unfiltered moments with close friends, and other times curating more intentional, artistic collections to express themselves to a broader audience. The current app didn’t support this duality.

I mapped out the complete user journey to identify key interaction points and optimize the user flow.

The Ideation & Strategy

Based on my research insights, I developed a comprehensive strategy that balances user needs with technical feasibility. My ideation process focused on creating solutions that would feel natural and engaging while addressing the core challenges I identified.

The Solution

The new Polaroid app experience was designed to embrace the dual nature of creativity, offering users the ability to capture and share both raw, everyday moments and thoughtfully curated collections. With social walls, collaborative albums, and an intuitive browsing flow, the app becomes a space where connection, expression, and creativity coexist seamlessly.

The new navigation introduces four core sections—Home, Capture, Walls, and Profile—to streamline how users interact with the Polaroid app. Home curates Polaroids from friends, public walls, and challenges, keeping users inspired and connected. Capture allows for quick photo uploads, editing, and sharing. Walls introduces collaborative albums, letting users create or explore shared collections with friends or the community. Profile brings together personal Polaroids, wall contributions, and saved inspirations.

This navigation solves the user’s problem of disconnected sharing and discovery by making it easier to create, explore, and connect—right from the start.

The Capture screen allows users to either scan existing Polaroids or upload them from their device. Once uploaded, they can tag people, write a caption, and choose where to post it — on their personal wall, in a shared album, or just save it privately.

This solves the user's problem of making physical moments easy to preserve and share digitally, without losing the emotional touch of the Polaroid brand.

The Walls screen is where users organize and showcase their Polaroids into thematic collections — like personal albums, trips, or shared memories. Users can create private or collaborative walls, invite friends or family, and choose whether to make them public.

This feature solves the user's problem of storing and sharing Polaroids in a way that feels intentional, creative, and connected — replacing the limitations of traditional albums with dynamic, living memory boards.

The Home screen features a scrollable, visually rich feed of Polaroid Moments — real photos from other users, curated walls, and community highlights. It offers a seamless way to explore and get inspired by how others are capturing and sharing their lives.

This solves the user’s problem of feeling isolated in the app by creating a more social, discovery-focused experience that reflects the emotional and analog spirit of the Polaroid brand.

Results & Possible Impact

The redesigned app introduced new ways to capture, explore, and share Polaroids, creating a more connected, creative experience. By integrating social discovery and personal expression, the platform now fosters stronger engagement, encourages community building, and aligns more closely with Polaroid's analog, emotional identity.

Key Learnings

  • Nostalgia sells: Users value emotional expression and want flexible ways to share moments, both publicly and privately.
  • Brand Consistency: A strong brand identity should extend into digital touchpoints to keep the experience cohesive.
